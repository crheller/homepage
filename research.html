Overview

In my current research, I study how changes in behavioral context affect the neural population coding of sounds in auditory cortex. To do this, I use multi-site laminar electrodes to record neural population activity from the auditory cortex of awake, behaving animals while presenting sound stimuli. I use computational analyses to determine how the neural representation of sound changes as a function of behavioral context.

With this work, I hope to gain fundamental insights about neural processing that will ultimately contribute to the improvement of engineered systems for sensory signal processing.
