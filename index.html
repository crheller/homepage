About Me

I am currently a PhD student in the Neuroscience Graduate Program at Oregon Health and Science University. I am pursuing my thesis work in the Laboratory of Brain Hearing and Behavior under the supervision of Dr. Stephen David. My graduate studies are supported through a National Science Foundation Graduate Research Fellowship.

My research interest is neural population coding. I am particularly interested in how behavioral state modulates neural processing of sensory stimuli. For more on my current research, see my research page.

Education

I received my B.A. in Physics from St. Olaf College in the spring of 2016.
